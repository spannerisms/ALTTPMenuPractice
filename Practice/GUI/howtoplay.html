Gameplay

The goal of Menu Simulator 2K17 is to train your Link to the Past menuing skills by training your brain to find the optimal path between two items and training your hands to execute it as quickly as possible.

Items are randomly chosen to be placed on the menu, with some items e.g., bombs and bottles appearing more often, simply because there are more chances to find them when actually playing.

Your current selection is indicated with a green circle, and your target is indicated with two green rings.

To confirm your selection, press START.

Game modes

Study

In Study, you are given a brief period of time to study the layout of the menu before playing a round.

Blitz

In Blitz, the layout of the menu changes every time.

Collections

In Collections, you start with 4 random items and gain more as the round increases. It's like playing randomizer, but without all the boring stuff. Just good ol' menuing.

Difficulties

There are five difficulty settings:

  • Beginner
  • Easy
  • Medium
  • Hard
  • Expert

Differences between modes:

  • All modes
    • Easier difficulties will change the menu up less often, giving you more time to get used to it.
    • Beginner will overlay an outline of the optimal path it found.
    • Beginner, Easy, and Medium have a cursor over the target item; hard and expert do not.
    • Expert will not show the target item\'s icon next to its name.
  • Study
    • Higher difficulties allot less time to study the menu before playing.
    • Menu layout changes more often in higher difficulties.
    • In medium and expert, your starting position changes each turn of the round.
  • Blitz
    • Higher difficulties have more rounds before the game ends.
  • Collections
    • Higher difficulties add items added more often.
    • In expert, your starting position is randomized each turn.

Scoring

After every round, you will receive a score to judge your performance based on the following factors:

  • Difficulty setting
  • The number of moves you made
  • How long it took you
  • A bonus for having an optimal path
  • A penalty for pressing START on the wrong item

All of your scores and moves are stored for later viewing. To see what you did on a specific turn, select the column in the table and click the "Recap" button. This will open a new dialog with the following additional information:

  • A snapshot of the original item layout, including the starting point and goal.
  • The optimal path found by the computer.
  • The path you took, indicated with hollow, blue arrows.
  • Any slot you pressed start on, indicated with two blue rings.
